Frequently Asked Questions

What is Cosmos Daily?

Cosmos Daily is a celestial history chronicle that maps major celestial events — eclipses, conjunctions, oppositions, and planetary ingresses — to the pivotal world events they coincide with. We span over 5,800 years of history, from the Rigveda Eclipse of 3811 BC to the present day, revealing the patterns between what happens in the sky and what happens on Earth.

Is this astrology?

Cosmos Daily draws from mundane astrology — the branch of astrology applied to world events, nations, and collective history rather than individual birth charts. We present verified historical correlations between celestial alignments and major events. Whether these correlations are causal, symbolic, or coincidental is left to the reader.

How are events selected and verified?

Every event in our database is historically verified using primary sources, academic records, and established chronologies. Celestial data comes from NASA's ephemeris tables and confirmed astronomical records. We cross-reference both to ensure accuracy. Events are rated on a 1–5 significance scale based on celestial rarity and historical impact.

What does the significance rating mean?

Each event is rated 1–5 based on celestial weight. Outer planet alignments (Saturn, Pluto, Neptune, Uranus) score higher than inner planet events. Conjunctions at cardinal points (0° Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn) receive the highest ratings. A significance-5 event like the Saturn-Pluto conjunction of 2020 is a once-in-a-generation alignment.

How are celestial event types ranked?

We categorize celestial events by six types, ranked by their historical impact:

  1. Eclipse — Most powerful. Triggers events for 6+ months. Eclipses act as cosmic spotlights, exposing hidden flaws and forcing change.
  2. Conjunction — New cycles begin. Two planets merge energy at the same degree, marking major shifts in the areas they govern.
  3. Opposition — Culmination and confrontation. 180° angles bring full expression — what began at the conjunction now reaches its peak or breaking point.
  4. Square — Crisis points. 90° angles create tension, friction, and forced action. Squares break what isn't working.
  5. Retrograde — Review, reversal, disruption. Planets appear to move backward, forcing reconsideration of what their domain governs.
  6. Ingress — A planet enters a new zodiac sign, beginning a gradual shift in collective energy. Outer planet ingresses shape entire generations.
What is the "What's Happening Now" section?

This section maps current world events to active celestial transits. Right now, the Saturn-Neptune conjunction at 0° Aries (institutional dissolution), Uranus in Gemini (communication revolution), and the Blood Moon in Virgo (systemic exposure) are the major active transits shaping global events in 2025–2026.

How often is the archive updated?

The historical archive contains over 120 events and grows as new celestial alignments activate. The "What's Happening Now" feed is updated to reflect current transits and their real-world correlations. Subscribe to our Cosmos Daily for weekly transit readings delivered to your inbox.

Celestial Glossary

◑ Eclipse
Solar or Lunar eclipse. Sudden revelations, endings, and the exposure of hidden truths. Historically tied to the fates of leaders and nations.
☌ Conjunction
Two planets at the same degree. Energies merge — beginnings, intensifications, new cycles.
☍ Opposition
Planets 180° apart. Peak tension, confrontation, culmination of opposing forces.
□ Square
Planets 90° apart. Friction and crisis demanding action. The most turbulent aspect.
℞ Retrograde
A planet appears to move backwards. Past issues resurface, systems break down, revisions and reckonings unfold.
↑ Ingress
A planet enters a new zodiac sign, beginning a new thematic chapter lasting years.
☉ Sun
The core identity, leadership, and vitality of nations. Solar eclipses strike at the heart of power — exposing leaders, toppling regimes, and forcing change at the highest levels.
☽ Moon
The people, public mood, and collective emotion. Lunar eclipses reveal hidden truths and trigger mass emotional responses — revolutions, panics, and collective awakenings.
☿ Mercury
Communication, trade, travel, and information. Mercury retrogrades disrupt technology, contracts, and negotiations. Governs media, journalism, and the flow of ideas.
♀ Venus
Diplomacy, alliances, finance, and culture. Venus transits shape treaties, trade agreements, and cultural movements. Governs art, beauty, and the value systems of civilizations.
♂ Mars
War, aggression, conflict, and decisive action. Mars transits trigger military operations, assassinations, and eruptions of violence. The planet of generals, revolutionaries, and conquerors.
♃ Jupiter
Expansion, law, religion, and empire. Jupiter transits bring growth, conquest, and the spread of ideology. Governs courts, universities, foreign affairs, and the ambition of nations.
♄ Saturn
Time, authority, structure, and consequence. Saturn transits test institutions — what is built on solid ground endures; what is corrupt collapses. The planet of governments, borders, and accountability.
♅ Uranus
Revolution, technology, and sudden disruption. Uranus transits shatter the status quo — triggering inventions, uprisings, and paradigm shifts. Governs electricity, aviation, computing, and freedom movements.
♆ Neptune
Dissolution, illusion, spirituality, and collective dreams. Neptune transits blur boundaries — empires dissolve, ideologies collapse, pandemics spread, and mass deception peaks before truth surfaces.
♇ Pluto
Death, transformation, power, and regeneration. Pluto transits destroy and rebuild from the ground up — empires fall, nuclear power emerges, hidden corruption is exposed. Cycles last 12–30 years per sign.
⚷ Chiron
The wounded healer. Chiron transits expose collective wounds — injustice, trauma, and systemic failures — forcing societies to confront what they have ignored and begin painful healing.
Cardinal Points
0° Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn — the four world axis degrees. Alignments at cardinal points have the strongest mundane impact, affecting nations and global events rather than individuals.
Mundane Astrology
The branch of astrology applied to world events, nations, and collective history rather than individual birth charts. The framework behind Cosmos Daily.
