# Cosmos Daily — Significance Rating Model

## Overview
Every event in the archive is rated 1-5 for significance. This rating directly controls card size in the grid layout. To maintain visual balance, significance must follow a strict distribution model.

## Rating Scale

| Rating | Label | Target % | Criteria |
|--------|-------|----------|----------|
| **5** | Civilizational | 12-15% | Changed the trajectory of human civilization permanently. Would appear on any "50 events that shaped the world" list. Must have strong celestial connection. |
| **4** | Major | 30-35% | Major historical event with documented celestial alignment. Regional or domain-defining impact. |
| **3** | Significant | 30-35% | Notable event with celestial link. Important within its era, category, or region. |
| **2** | Notable | 15-20% | Smaller scope or weaker celestial connection. Included for pattern completeness or niche significance. |
| **1** | Background | 0-5% | Minor entries. Rarely used. |

## The 5-Test (all three must be YES)
1. **Universal recognition** — Would a general audience immediately recognize this as world-changing?
2. **Civilizational impact** — Did it permanently alter the course of human civilization (not just one nation or domain)?
3. **Celestial strength** — Does it have a strong, documented celestial connection (eclipse, major conjunction, outer planet aspect)?

## Distribution Targets

| Archive Size | Rating 5 | Rating 4 | Rating 3 | Rating 2 |
|-------------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| 100 events | 12-15 | 30-35 | 30-35 | 15-20 |
| 150 events | 18-22 | 45-52 | 45-52 | 22-30 |
| 200 events | 24-30 | 60-70 | 60-70 | 30-40 |
| 300 events | 36-45 | 90-105 | 90-105 | 45-60 |

## Examples by Rating

### Rating 5 — Civilizational
- Fall of Rome (476) — End of Western civilization's first chapter
- Black Death (1347) — Killed 1/3 of Europe, ended feudalism
- WWII Begins (1939) — Deadliest conflict in human history
- Hiroshima (1945) — Nuclear age, existential threat to species
- Moon Landing (1969) — Humanity leaves Earth
- Fall of Soviet Union (1991) — End of Cold War, unipolar world

### Rating 4 — Major
- Sputnik (1957) — Space age begins, but built on by later events
- Civil Rights Act (1964) — Transformed one nation, not all civilization
- iPhone Launch (2007) — Revolutionary but evolutionary from existing tech
- Armenian Genocide (1915) — Horrific but regional; coined "genocide"
- Treaty of Westphalia (1648) — Birth of nation-state, but gradual impact

### Rating 3 — Significant
- Discovery of Pluto (1930) — Scientific milestone, limited civilizational impact
- Boxer Rebellion (1900) — Regional crisis
- Salem Witch Trials (1692) — Cultural event, local scope
- Prohibition (1920) — US-only policy experiment

## When Adding New Events
1. Rate conservatively — default to 3, upgrade only with strong justification
2. Check the current distribution before assigning 4 or 5
3. Ask: "Is this MORE significant than [existing event at same rating]?"
4. Never exceed 15% at rating 5
5. Always download the event image before pushing
