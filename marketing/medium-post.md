The timeline he keeps returning to: **2026**.

## Chris Bledsoe: The Star Rising Before the Sphinx

Chris Bledsoe's story comes from a completely different world. After a UFO encounter in 2007 that was investigated by NASA and CIA personnel, he claims ongoing contact with a luminous feminine being he calls "The Lady" — whom he identifies with the Egyptian goddess Hathor and various Marian apparitions.

On Easter 2012, Bledsoe says The Lady delivered a prophecy: when the star Regulus appears red on the horizon, in the gaze of the Sphinx, before daylight — that moment would mark a fundamental shift in human awareness.

Regulus is the brightest star in Leo — one of the four ancient Royal Stars of Persia, associated with kings, power shifts, and epochal change for over 5,000 years. The Great Sphinx of Giza faces due east, directly toward the rising point of Leo.

Astronomers using Stellarium and NASA's JPL ephemeris have calculated that the conditions Bledsoe described — Regulus rising due east before dawn at the latitude of the Sphinx — occur around **October 7, 2026**.

You don't have to believe in The Lady to find the astronomical specificity interesting. Bledsoe made this claim in 2012. The date falls in 2026. And it involves a star that ancient civilizations explicitly associated with the fate of kingdoms.

Mundane astrology is neither Jiang's structural analysis nor Bledsoe's prophetic vision. It's a pattern recognition system that has been documented since Babylon — roughly 2,500 years of continuous practice. It maps planetary orbital cycles to recurring types of historical events.
